Check & Mate (INTRA & INTER)

Ever since its inception in India chess has been one of the popular strategic sport. Played on an 8X8 grid with 16 pieces of blacks and whites with each player who tries to become the last one standing on the board.

Venue(Intra & Inter) - Admission Cell, PIET

Reporting Time(Intra & Inter) - 09:00 A.M.

Date - 22/01/2020(Intra) & 23/01/2020(Inter)

Rules And Regultions
  1. No talking or making comments on any games/moves.
  2. If opponent is late you must wait a time period equal to the time control of the game up to 60 minutes as measured by your clock, you may then claim a win on time.
  3. If both players arrive late, the first player to set up the equipment should split the elapsed time and then start his opponent’s clock.
  4. If you touch a piece, you must move it. If you touch an opponent’s piece, you must take it.
  5. If there is a dispute, or if a player wishes to make a claim of any kind, the player should stop both clocks, advise your opponent of your claim, and summon a event coordinator.
  6. Pawn promotion. It is improper to punch your clock while the pawn is still on the board. As soon as the new piece is placed on board punch your clock. Improper punching of clock will be penalized one minute added to opponent’s time (at Director’s discretion).
  7. Any player absents from the playing room or board for more than fifteen minutes without permission from the tournament coordinator may have his or her game forfeited due to unsportsmanlike conduct (at coordinators discretion).
  8. Understand the following rules prior to the tournament.
    • Checkmate
    • Pawn promotion
    • Castling and End Passant
    • 50 moves draw
    • 3-fold repetition rule

Table Tennis (INTRA)

Ping-pong match played between 2 players, each trying to slip the ball from their opponent to win the game.

Venue(Intra) - Basement Civil DEPT,Central Block, PCE

Reporting Time(Intra) - 09:00 A.M.

Date - 21/01/2020

Rules And Regultions
  1. The ball must be thrown vertically, at least 16 cm.
  2. The ball must be above and behind the table throughout the serve.
  3. After throwing the ball, the server must get their free arm and hand out of the way. This is to allow the receiver to see the ball.
  4. You have two serves before your opponent’s turn to serve twice. This used to be five serves each but since changing to 11 it’s now just two.
  5. At 10-10 it’s deuce. You get one serve each and must win by two clear points. This is sudden death or table tennis’ equivalent of a tie break.
  6. If you are playing a best of 3, 5 or 7 (as opposed to just one set) you have to change ends after each game. This makes sure both players experience conditions on both sides of the table. You also change ends when the first player reaches five points in the final game of a match.
  7. The final decision will be taken by the judges present over their.

A Point If lost..

  1. The service is missed.
  2. The service is not returned.
  3. A shot goes into the net.
  4. A shot goes off the table without touching the court.
  5. A player moves the table, touches the net or touches the table with their free hand during play

A let is called if..

  1. An otherwise good serve touches the net. This ensures your opponent has a chance at making a return.
  2. The receiver isn’t ready (and doesn’t try to hit the ball). This is just common sense really!
  3. If play is disturbed by something outside of the players’ control. This allows you to replay the point if your cat jumps onto the table.

Basketball Boys (INTRA)

An exquisite game played between 2 teams, each comprising of 8 male members. Each team strives to throw the ball in the hoop to procure enough points to be triumphant.

Venue(Intra) - Basketball Court, PIET

Reporting Time(Intra) - 09:00 A.M.

Date - 21/01/2020

Rules And Regultions
  1. Two teams of maximum 5+3 players, with a maximum of five players of each team on the court at any time.
  2. The game consists of four periods of 8 minutes and 10 minutes in finals.
  3. If the scores are tied, overtime periods of five minutes will be played until one team has more points than the other (at the end of the 5-minute period)
  4. A basket scored from near the basket (inside the three-point arc) is worth two points.
  5. A basket scored from far (beyond the three-point arc) is worth three points.
  6. A basket scored from the free-throw line is worth one point.
  7. Once the team in possession of the ball has passed the half-court line, it may not cross back over the line with the ball.
  8. Additionally, offensive players may not remain within the restricted area (key) for more than three consecutive seconds.
  9. A foul made on a player attempting a shot results in the awarding of the same number of free-throws as those of the shot taken (two from inside the arc, three from outside). If a player is fouled but makes the attempted shot, the shot counts and an additional free-throw will be awarded.
  10. Once a team has made four fouls in a period, each additional foul (on a player not attempting a shot) will result in the automatic awarding of two free-throws.

4*100m Boys Relay (INTRA)

4 boys each sprinting for 100m before passing the baton to their teammates. The team crossing the finish line first wins the race.

Venue(Intra) - Open Ground Infront PCE

Reporting Time(Intra) - 09:30 A.M.

Date - 22/01/2020

Rules And Regultions
  1. During this race, each runner runs 100 meters with a baton in-hand.
  2. Each runner runs the same distance throughout the race.
  3. That teammate must receive the baton within a 20-meter area.
  4. If the pass comes before or after that area, the pass is considered illegal, and the team is disqualified.
  5. Also, if a runner drops the baton while passing or in the middle of the race, the team is disqualied.
  6. The team that completes four separate baton passes legally and successfully and has its last runner cross the finish line first wins the race.
